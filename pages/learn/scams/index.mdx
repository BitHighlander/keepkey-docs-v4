Based on the search results, here are the most common signs of a crypto scam:

1. Unsolicited offers: You receive an offer out of the blue, often via email, text message, or social media[1][8].

2. Promises of guaranteed returns: Any investment claiming to guarantee profits is likely fraudulent[2][4].

3. Pressure to act quickly: Scammers create a false sense of urgency to push you into making hasty decisions[6].

4. Celebrity endorsements: Be wary of celebrity advertisements or endorsements, as they are often fake[1][5].

5. Requests for cryptocurrency payments: Scammers may ask you to pay for financial services or solve problems using cryptocurrency[1][3].

6. Poorly written or non-existent whitepapers: Legitimate cryptocurrencies should have well-written, comprehensive whitepapers[4].

7. Excessive marketing: Be cautious of cryptocurrencies with aggressive marketing campaigns[2].

8. Phishing attempts: Watch out for emails or websites asking for your private keys or wallet information[2][4].

9. Fake apps and websites: Scammers create counterfeit apps and websites to steal your information or cryptocurrency[4][7].

10. Romantic partners asking for money: Be suspicious if an online romantic interest requests cryptocurrency transfers[1].

11. Pressure to transfer crypto to another website: Legitimate exchanges won't force you to move your assets[1].

12. Blackmail or extortion attempts: Be wary of threats to expose personal information unless you send cryptocurrency[3][4].

By being aware of these signs, you can better protect yourself from falling victim to cryptocurrency scams.
